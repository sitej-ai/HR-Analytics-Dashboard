# HR Analytics Dashboard

An interactive **HR Analytics Dashboard** built in **Power BI** to analyze employee attrition, workforce demographics, salary trends, and job satisfaction across different departments. This dashboard helps HR professionals identify key factors contributing to employee attrition and supports data-driven decision-making.

---

## Project Overview

The HR Analytics Dashboard provides a comprehensive overview of the organization's workforce by analyzing employee demographics, attrition trends, job roles, education fields, and departmental performance.

The dashboard enables users to:

- Monitor overall workforce statistics
- Analyze employee attrition
- Compare departments
- Filter data by Gender and Department
- Understand attrition across age groups, education fields, and job roles
- Support strategic HR planning

---

## Dashboard Features

### KPI Cards

The dashboard displays important HR metrics including:

- Total Employees
- Employee Attrition
- Attrition Percentage
- Average Years at Company
- Average Employee Age
- Average Salary

---

### Visualizations

#### Employee Attrition by Age Group

- Identifies which age groups experience the highest employee attrition.

#### Employee Attrition by Years at Company

- Displays attrition trends based on employee tenure.

#### Employee Attrition by Education Field

- Shows the distribution of employee attrition across different educational backgrounds.

#### Employee Attrition by Job Role & Job Satisfaction

- Compares attrition among various job roles.
- Includes Job Satisfaction ratings (1–4).

---

### Interactive Filters

Users can dynamically filter the dashboard by:

- Gender
  - Female
  - Male

- Department
  - Human Resources
  - Research & Development
  - Sales

---

## Key Insights

Some business insights obtained from the dashboard include:

- Employees aged **26–35** show the highest attrition.
- Research & Development contains the largest workforce.
- Attrition varies significantly across departments.
- Certain job roles experience higher employee turnover.
- Job satisfaction levels have a visible relationship with attrition.
- Life Sciences and Medical education backgrounds represent a large portion of employee attrition.
